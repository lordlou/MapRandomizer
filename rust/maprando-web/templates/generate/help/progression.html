Item progression

Item progression settings affect the rate at which the randomizer will place key items, and which locations it will place them in, within the constraints of the selected "Skill assumptions" above. Three presets are available:

  • Normal: Key items are placed generously: by the time 50% of items are obtainable, at least 75% of key items should be obtainable. Morph and an ETank are placed early. Key items have no specific tendency to be in harder or easier locations to reach. Beams, additional tanks, and ammo are spread out uniformly. Multiple progression options are generally possible from relatively early in the game.

  • Tricky: Key items are spread out at a roughly uniform pace but tend to be placed in harder-to-reach locations. Beams, tanks, and ammo are spread out uniformly. Multiple progression options are generally possible by the middle of the game.

  • Challenge: Key items are delayed and tend to be placed in harder-to-reach locations. A single progression sequence may be forced until late in the game. Access to tanks and ammo other than Missiles is often delayed.

  • Desolate: Some key items may not be placed at all, and the amount of ammo and tanks is greatly reduced. A single progression sequence may be forced through almost the entire game.

For players new to this randomizer, the "Normal" preset is highly recommended. The "Tricky" preset tends to make the game quite a bit more difficult. For "Challenge" and "Desolate" it can be much, much more difficult.

"Item progression" and "Skill assumptions" synergize with one another: harder item progression settings increase the likelihood that harder techniques selected in the "Skill assumptions" will actually be required to beat the game.

The item progression settings can be tweaked by expanding the "Customize" section below. Note that selecting a preset again will override any customized selections.
